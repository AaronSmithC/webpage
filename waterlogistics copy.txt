  <h1 style="font-size: 2.5em; margin-bottom: 0.2em;">Lunar Water Logistics</h1>
  <p style="font-size: 1.2em; margin-bottom: 2em;">
    Supporting the completion of Aegis Station and establishing the first off-Earth water economy.
  </p>

  

  <!-- Master Dossier Content -->
  <h2>1. Executive Summary</h2>
  <p>
    Aegis Station's three-ring configuration and central hub require over <strong>400,000 metric tons of water</strong>—equivalent to <strong>163 Olympic swimming pools</strong>—to complete its radiation shielding and life-support reserves. Hauling that volume from Earth would be prohibitively expensive and logistically impractical. Instead, the station will be filled entirely with <strong>lunar-sourced water</strong>, mined and lifted from the Moon’s surface.
  </p>
  <p>
    Our logistics baseline calls for a <strong>fleet of 20 reusable tankers</strong>, each delivering <strong>15 tons of water per day</strong> from lunar surface to orbit. This system will fill Aegis Station in <strong>just under four years</strong> and continue operating beyond that point to support:
  </p>
  <ul>
    <li>Fuel production (via electrolysis: H₂ + O₂)</li>
    <li>Orbital depot refueling (cislunar and LEO)</li>
    <li>Life support reserves for long-duration crews</li>
    <li>Shielding and utility water for future habitats or spacecraft</li>
  </ul>
  <p>
    The water economy doesn’t end with Aegis—it begins with it.
  </p>

  <h2>2. Water Requirement Overview</h2>
  <p>
    Total water volume: <strong>406,848 m³</strong> (~163 Olympic pools). This covers three shielding rings and the central hub.
  </p>
  <ul>
    <li>Ring Shields (×3): ~315,200 m³</li>
    <li>Central Hub: ~91,648 m³</li>
    <li><strong>Total Mass:</strong> 406,848 metric tons</li>
  </ul>

  <h2>3. Delivery Architecture</h2>
  <ul>
    <li>Fleet: 20 reusable tankers</li>
    <li>Payload: 15 tons each</li>
    <li>Daily Throughput: 300 tons/day</li>
    <li>Fill Time: ~3.7 years</li>
    <li>Estimated Trips: ~27,123</li>
  </ul>

  <h2>4. Lunar ISRU Operations</h2>
  <p>
    Water is extracted from permanently shadowed craters near the lunar south pole using microwave heating and thermal processing. Collected vapor is condensed, purified, and loaded into cryogenic tanks.
  </p>
  <ul>
    <li>Excavation and thermal separation</li>
    <li>Solar/nuclear power sources</li>
    <li>Autonomous mining and transfer systems</li>
  </ul>
  <p>[diagram here: ISRU process flow]</p>
