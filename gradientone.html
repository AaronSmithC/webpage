Gradient One

The First Floor with Gravity

Overview

Gradient One is a full-scale, human-rated orbital segment designed to validate artificial gravity. Featuring a 350-meter rigid truss and an 80-meter-diameter curved pod, it rotates at ~1.6 RPM to generate 1g at the floor—exactly matching the geometry of Aegis Station.

Specifications

  • Truss Length: 350 meters
  • Spin Rate: ~1.6 RPM
  • Gravity: ~1g at floor, ~0.86g at ceiling
  • Crew Pod: 5° torus segment, 80m diameter
  • Walking Arc: ~30.6 meters
  • Duration: 30–90 day missions
  • Access: EVA-based (tunnel upgrade interface reserved)
  • Target Cost: $65M–67M
